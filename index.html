OCR

OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It is a technology that enables the conversion of printed text image into machine-readable text. OCR systems use a combination of hardware (such as scanners or cameras) and software to recognize and extract text from images or documents. The process of OCR involves several steps. First, the image or document containing the text is captured using a scanner or camera. Then, the OCR software analyzes the image and identifies individual characters, words, or paragraphs. It uses various techniques like pattern recognition, feature detection, and machine learning algorithms to recognize and interpret the text.

TTS

ext-to-speech (TTS) is a technology that converts written text into spoken words. It allows computers or devices to read out text in a natural-sounding voice, giving the impression that a human is speaking. The process of text-to-speech involves several steps. First, the written text is analyzed and processed by TTS software. This software breaks down the text into individual words, sentences, and phrases, and applies linguistic rules and algorithms to determine pronunciation, intonation, and emphasis. Once the text is processed, the TTS system uses a synthesized voice to generate the corresponding speech. The synthesized voice is created using pre-recorded human speech samples or through the use of advanced algorithms that simulate human speech patterns. These algorithms take into account factors such as pitch, tone, and rhythm to create a natural-sounding voice.
